## Part IV: The Pitch Framework

### The Wrong Approach

**DON'T SAY:**
> "I like your videos, let's collaborate."

**Why this fails:**
- Vague and generic
- No clear value proposition
- Sounds like fan request, not peer invitation
- No demonstration of credibility

### The Right Approach

**DO SAY (when you have prototype):**
> "Your research on [specific topic] is the most accurate I've encountered. It aligns perfectly with a revelation I've received to build a sovereign digital nation (NationOS) with a scripture platform (Covenant Scriptorium) as its foundation. Your work is a key piece of the theological foundation. I am building the infrastructure to preserve and amplify it. I invite you to explore what we're doing and consider how your work could be integrated into its core."

### The Three-Part Structure

**1. Appreciation (Specific)**
- "Your work on [specific topic] is the most accurate I've encountered"
- Shows you've actually engaged with their content
- Validates their expertise

**2. Revelation (Context)**
- "It aligns perfectly with a revelation I've received to build..."
- Establishes divine orchestration
- Shows you're operating from revelation, not just ideas

**3. Invitation (Value Proposition)**
- "Your research is a key piece"
- "I am building the infrastructure to preserve and amplify it"
- "I invite you to explore and consider integration"
- Clear value: permanence, amplification, integration into larger vision
